Author Guidelines

Thank you for considering Catalyst Journals for your research publication. These guidelines provide essential information for preparing and submitting your manuscript. Please read them carefully before submission.

Note: Submissions that do not follow these guidelines may be returned to authors for correction before peer review.

Manuscript Preparation

General Structure

All manuscripts should include the following sections in order:

  1. Title: Concise and informative (max 20 words)
  2. Authors: Full names and affiliations
  3. Abstract: 200-250 words, structured for research articles (Background, Methods, Results, Conclusions)
  4. Keywords: 5-8 keywords
  5. Introduction: Context and objectives
  6. Methods: Sufficient detail for reproducibility
  7. Results and Discussion: May be combined or separate
  8. Conclusions: Main findings and significance
  9. Acknowledgments: Funding sources and contributions
  10. References: Formatted according to journal style

Formatting Requirements

Text

  • Microsoft Word (.docx) format
  • 12 pt Times New Roman font
  • 1.5 line spacing
  • 2.5 cm margins on all sides
  • Page numbers in footer

Figures & Tables

  • High resolution (300 dpi minimum)
  • TIFF, EPS, or PDF format
  • Numbered sequentially
  • Captions below figures, above tables
  • Embedded in text and submitted separately

Supplementary Materials

Additional data, methods, or multimedia files may be submitted as supplementary materials. These should be:

  • Clearly labeled (e.g., "Supplementary Figure S1")
  • Referenced in the main text
  • Submitted as separate files
  • Formatted appropriately for their content type

Citation Style

Catalyst Journals uses the American Chemical Society (ACS) citation style. Examples:

In-text citations:

• Sequential numbers in superscript: The reaction proceeded efficiently1,2

• Or in parentheses: The reaction proceeded efficiently (1, 2)

Reference list examples:

Journal Article: 1. Author, A.; Author, B. J. Abbrev. Year, Volume, Page Range.

Book: 2. Author, A. Book Title; Publisher: Location, Year; Vol. 3, pp 100-150.

Website: 3. Organization. Page Title. URL (accessed Month Day, Year).

Download citation style files for EndNote, Zotero, and Mendeley

Ethical Guidelines

Originality and Plagiarism

All submissions must be original work. Any text or ideas from other sources must be properly cited. Plagiarism in any form is unacceptable.

Authorship

All listed authors must have made significant contributions to the research. Changes to authorship after submission require approval from all authors.

Data Integrity

Authors may be asked to provide raw data for editorial review. Fabrication or falsification of data constitutes misconduct.

Conflict of Interest

All potential conflicts of interest must be disclosed upon submission.

Submission Guidelines

Manuscript Template

While using our official template is highly recommended, it is not mandatory. The template ensures proper formatting and includes all required sections, which can speed up the review process. If you don't use the template, ensure your manuscript follows our formatting guidelines exactly:

  • Double-spaced, 12-point Times New Roman font
  • 1-inch margins on all sides
  • Line numbers throughout the document
  • Proper heading structure and reference format

Download the template: BJCET Manuscript Template

Word and Page Limits

We have established the following word limits for different manuscript types:

  • Research Articles: 8,000-12,000 words (excluding references)
  • Review Articles: 10,000-15,000 words (excluding references)
  • Short Communications: 3,000-5,000 words (excluding references)
  • Technical Notes: 2,000-4,000 words (excluding references)
  • Abstract: 250-300 words maximum
  • Keywords: 5-8 keywords maximum

Note: Manuscripts significantly exceeding these limits may be subject to desk rejection or require substantial revision.

Accepted File Formats

We accept manuscripts in the following formats:

  • Main Document: Microsoft Word (.docx) or LaTeX (.tex with PDF)
  • Figures: High-resolution TIFF, PNG, or EPS (minimum 300 DPI)
  • Tables: Embedded in main document or separate .docx files
  • Supplementary Materials: PDF, Word, Excel, or multimedia files

APC payment is required only after your manuscript is accepted for publication. The sequence is:

  1. You submit your manuscript (no payment required)
  2. Manuscript undergoes peer review
  3. If accepted, you receive an acceptance letter with invoice
  4. You make the payment within 30 days
  5. Article is published after payment confirmation

Review Process

Review Timeline

Our typical review timeline is:

  • Initial Editorial Screening: 5-7 business days
  • Peer Review: 6-8 weeks
  • Author Revision: 4-6 weeks (if required)
  • Final Decision: 2-4 weeks after revision
  • Total Time: 10-15 weeks from submission to final decision

Manuscript Status Tracking

You can track your manuscript status 24/7 through:

  • Your submission portal dashboard
  • Email notifications at key milestones
  • Direct email inquiry to the editorial office

Common status updates include: Submitted, Under Review, Revision Required, Accepted, Payment Pending, In Production, Published.
